Early Life

Virat Kohli was born on 5 November 1988 in Delhi. He grew up in a middle-class Punjabi family in the capital city of India. His father Prem Kohli worked as a criminal lawyer and his mother Saroj Kohli is a homemaker. Virat has an elder brother named Vikas Kohli and an elder sister named Bhavna Kohli. From a very young age, Virat showed a strong interest in cricket and loved playing the sport with great enthusiasm. When Virat was just three years old, he started holding a cricket bat and playing with it at home. He often asked his father to bowl to him so that he could practice batting. His father noticed his passion and talent for cricket at an early age. Realizing his son's interest in the game, his father decided to provide him with proper training. At the age of nine, Virat joined the West Delhi Cricket Academy. There he received professional coaching and began learning the techniques of cricket. At the academy, Virat was trained by coach Rajkumar Sharma. Under his guidance, Virat improved his batting skills and developed a strong technique. He practiced for many hours every day and worked very hard to become a better player. His coach and teammates admired his dedication and determination. Virat soon became known as a talented young cricketer in Delhi. Virat completed his early education at Vishal Bharti Public School. Later, he joined Saviour Convent School, which supported students interested in sports. This allowed him to focus more on cricket training and participate in more matches. During his school years, Virat played many cricket tournaments and performed very well. His batting skills and confidence on the field helped him stand out among other young players. Because of his excellent performances, Virat was selected for the Delhi Under-15 cricket team. This was an important step in his cricket journey. He continued to perform well and later played for the Delhi Under-17 team. Virat scored many runs in youth tournaments and gained recognition for his talent. His aggressive batting style and confidence made him one of the most promising young players in Delhi cricket. In 2006, Virat experienced a very difficult moment in his life when his father passed away due to a brain stroke. This was a heartbreaking time for him and his family. However, Virat showed great courage and determination. Despite the personal loss, he continued playing cricket and even played an important Ranji Trophy match for Delhi the very next day. His dedication and commitment to the sport impressed many people. Virat's early life was full of hard work, discipline, and determination. He spent many years practicing and improving his cricket skills. His family, coaches, and teachers supported him throughout his journey. These experiences helped him develop a strong personality and a never-give-up attitude. His early struggles and dedication laid the foundation for his future success in international cricket. Later, he went on to become one of the greatest batsmen in the world and a key player for the Indian cricket team.Virat Kohli continued to work hard to achieve success in cricket and remained focused on improving his skills. His strong determination and dedication helped him become one of the most promising young cricketers in India. In 2008, he received a major opportunity when he was selected as the captain of the Indian Under-19 cricket team for the ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup held in Malaysia. Under his leadership, the Indian team performed brilliantly and won the tournament, bringing great pride to the country. This victory brought Virat Kohli into the spotlight and helped him gain recognition in the cricket world. Soon after the tournament, he started getting opportunities to play domestic cricket for Delhi. He played in several Ranji Trophy matches and performed consistently well. His aggressive batting style, confidence, and ability to score runs under pressure made him stand out among other players. Later in 2008, Virat Kohli was selected for the Indian national team and made his international debut in One Day Internationals against Sri Lanka. In the beginning, he faced tough competition from experienced players, but he remained patient and continued to work hard. Gradually, he improved his performances and secured his place in the team. In 2009, he scored his first international century, which was an important milestone in his career. Fans and cricket experts began to admire his fearless batting and his ability to chase big targets successfully. Over time, Virat became known as one of the best chasers in limited-overs cricket. His consistent performances helped India win many important matches. With every match, his confidence and experience continued to grow, making him a key player in the Indian cricket team.

Cricket Career

Virat Kohli began his international cricket career in 2008 when he was selected for the Indian national team. His debut match was a One Day International (ODI) against Sri Lanka national cricket team. At that time, he was a young and promising cricketer who had already gained attention for his strong performances in domestic cricket and the Under-19 team. Earlier in 2008, Kohli had led the India national under-19 cricket team to victory in the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup, which made him popular among cricket fans and selectors. In the early years of his international career, Kohli mainly played in ODI matches. He worked hard to secure a permanent place in the Indian team. His consistent performances and ability to score runs under pressure helped him become an important member of the team. In 2009, he scored his first international century against Sri Lanka national cricket team. This century proved his potential as a future star of Indian cricket. Kohli made his Test debut in 2011 against the West Indies cricket team. Although his early Test performances were not very strong, he soon improved his technique and became one of the best Test batsmen in the world. His determination and strong batting skills helped him score many centuries in Test cricket. Over time, he became one of the most reliable batsmen in the Indian Test team. In 2011, Kohli was also part of the Indian team that won the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup. This was one of the most important achievements of his early career. During the tournament, he scored a century in his World Cup debut match against Bangladesh national cricket team. Winning the World Cup was a historic moment for the Indian team and for Kohli as a young player. Kohli also made his T20 International debut in 2010 against the Zimbabwe national cricket team. In the shortest format of the game, he quickly became known for his aggressive batting style and excellent run-chasing ability. His calmness under pressure and ability to finish matches made him one of the most valuable players in the Indian team. Over the years, Kohli scored many centuries in all formats of the game. His batting technique, fitness, and mental strength helped him achieve great success. He became known for chasing targets successfully in ODI matches and earned the nickname “Chase Master.” Cricket experts and fans admired his passion, energy, and competitive spirit on the field. In 2013, Kohli was appointed as the vice-captain of the Indian ODI team. Later, he became the captain of the Indian team in all formats. Under his leadership, India achieved many victories in international cricket. Kohli focused on improving the teams fitness and performance standards. His leadership style was aggressive and motivating. One of Kohli’s greatest achievements as captain was leading India to the number one position in the ICC Test rankings. The Indian team performed strongly in Test cricket during his captaincy. He also led the team in many international tournaments and bilateral series around the world. Throughout his international career, Kohli broke many records. He became one of the fastest batsmen to score 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, and 12,000 runs in ODIs. He also scored a large number of centuries in international cricket. His consistent performances made him one of the greatest batsmen of the modern era. Kohli has also performed exceptionally well in major tournaments such as the ICC Cricket World Cup and the ICC T20 World Cup. In the 2023 ICC Cricket World Cup, he scored the highest number of runs in the tournament and broke several batting records. Apart from international cricket, Kohli is also a key player in the Indian Premier League (IPL), where he has played for the Royal Challengers Bengaluru since the beginning of the league. His performances in the IPL have also contributed to his reputation as one of the best batsmen in the world. Kohli stepped down as the captain of India’s T20I team in 2021 and later left the Test captaincy in 2022. However, he continues to play an important role in the Indian team as a senior and experienced player. His passion for cricket and dedication to the sport remain strong. Today, Virat Kohli is widely regarded as one of the greatest cricketers in the history of the game. His international cricket career is known for remarkable consistency, numerous records, and outstanding achievements. His journey from a young cricketer in Delhi to a global cricket superstar has inspired millions of fans around the world.

Personal Life

Virat Kohli is not only known for his achievements in cricket but also for his interesting and inspiring personal life. He was born on 5 November 1988 in Delhi and grew up in a loving family. His father Prem Kohli worked as a criminal lawyer and played an important role in supporting Virat’s cricket dreams. His mother Saroj Kohli is a homemaker who always encouraged him to work hard and stay disciplined. Virat also has an elder brother named Vikas Kohli and an elder sister named Bhavna Kohli. His family has always been a strong support system in his life. During his childhood, Virat was very energetic and passionate about cricket. Even outside the cricket field, he was known for his lively personality. His family noticed his determination and helped him follow his dream of becoming a professional cricketer. After the death of his father in 2006, Virat became emotionally stronger and more responsible. This difficult moment made him more focused on his career and his family. In his personal life, Virat Kohli is also famous for his relationship with Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma. The couple first met during the shooting of a television advertisement in 2013. Over time, their friendship grew into a strong relationship. They became one of the most popular celebrity couples in India. After several years of dating, Virat Kohli and Anushka Sharma got married on 11 December 2017 in a private ceremony in Tuscany. Their wedding was widely celebrated by fans and media across the country. The couple welcomed their first child, a daughter named Vamika, in January 2021. Later, they welcomed their second child, a son named Akaay, in February 2024. Virat often shares how becoming a father changed his life and made him more responsible and emotionally balanced. He values spending quality time with his family whenever he is not playing cricket. Virat Kohli is also well known for his dedication to fitness and a healthy lifestyle. He follows a strict workout routine and balanced diet. Over the years, he has inspired many young people to focus on fitness and health. Kohli also follows a vegetarian diet and promotes healthy living through his social media platforms. Apart from cricket, Virat has many other interests. He enjoys listening to music, traveling, and spending time with close friends and family. He is also passionate about fashion and owns clothing brands and businesses. His popularity and style have made him one of the most influential sports personalities in the world. Virat Kohli is also involved in charitable activities. He established the Virat Kohli Foundation, which works to support underprivileged children and promote sports among young people. Through this foundation, he helps provide education, sports training, and opportunities to talented young athletes. Despite his global fame and success, Virat is known for being very emotional and expressive. He often speaks about the importance of family, discipline, and hard work. His personal life reflects values such as dedication, resilience, and compassion. Today, Virat Kohli is admired not only as a great cricketer but also as a loving husband, caring father, and responsible public figure. His personal life continues to inspire millions of fans around the world, showing that success comes with hard work, strong values, and the support of loved ones.
